Ongoing Research

My ongoing research is focused on bioformatics and the intersection of pancreatic disease and microbiota. I am investigating how the pancreas interacts with microbial communities and how these communities influence disease progression and outcome. This involves developing methodologies and utilizing bioinformatics tools, metagenomic sequencing, machine learning techniques, and animal models to understand the role of the human microbiome in chronic and infectious diseases, such as pancreatic diseases.

  • Bioinformatics and Machine Learning
  • Host-Microbiota Interactions
  • Microbiome-Based Therapeutics

Past Research

Previously, I have focused on understanding the role of human microbiome in chronic (e.g., Inflammatory bowel disease, Post-traumatic stress disorder), and and infectious (e.g., Clostridioides difficile infection, COVID-19, and Urinary tract infection) diseases. My work has led to the discovery of novel microbial species with therapeutic potential and insights into how these microbes influence disease progression.

Collaborations

I am collaborating with various research groups to further investigate how microbial communities can be modulated to prevent or treat human diseases I also plan to explore novel computational methods to predict microbial effects on human health.
