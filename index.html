Bioinformatics and Machine Learning

Our lab specializes in bioinformatics and machine learning approaches to analyze microbiome data. We develop computational pipelines to process metagenomic sequencing data, identify microbial signatures associated with disease states, and apply machine learning techniques to predict disease outcomes and therapeutic responses.

Host-Microbiota Interactions

We investigate how host-microbiota interactions shape health and disease, focusing on immune responses, metabolite production, and pathogen resistance. Our research integrates multi-omics data to uncover key microbial players in gastrointestinal diseases, such as inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) and pancreatic cancer.

Microbiome-Based Therapeutics

We aim to translate microbiome research into clinical applications by developing microbiome-based therapeutics. Our work involves identifying beneficial microbial strains, engineering probiotics, and testing novel microbial interventions for gastrointestinal and systemic diseases.
